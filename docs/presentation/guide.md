## Narrative Logic & Reasoning

### Why This Specific Story Arc?

Our presentation follows a **Problem → Analysis → Solution** structure, but we've designed it to mirror how executives actually make decisions:

#### Stage 1: Establish Urgency (The Damage)
**Charts: 1 → 2 → 3 → 4**

**Logic:** Start with the most emotionally impactful number ($73.6M total loss), then systematically narrow focus.

| Step | Why This Order |
|------|----------------|
| cost_by_system | Executives think in business units first. "Which team owns this problem?" |
| cost_by_region | Expands to geography - shows this isn't a single team's failure |
| pareto_analysis | **Critical pivot point** - transforms overwhelming data into actionable focus |
| top10_system_region | Gives executives exactly 10 things to fix, not 100 |

**Psychological principle:** Move from overwhelming → manageable. The Pareto chart is the "relief moment" - we're not asking them to fix everything.

#### Stage 2: Build the Case (Root Cause)
**Charts: 5 → 6 → 7 → 8**

**Logic:** Answer "why is this happening?" with data, not opinions.

| Step | Why This Order |
|------|----------------|
| attack_type_distribution | Names the villain: Misconfiguration (not sophisticated hackers) |
| detection_response_heatmap | Shows operational failure - we can't even see attacks |
| cost_vs_detection | Proves causation, not just correlation |
| sensitivity_analysis | Quantifies why some data matters more |

**Psychological principle:** Build credibility through data before asking for money. Each chart answers the skeptic's "but how do you know that?"

#### Stage 3: Enable Decision (The Solution)
**Charts: 9 → 10**

**Logic:** Give them tools, not just findings.

| Step | Why This Order |
|------|----------------|
| risk_matrix | Visual decision tool - "where should my budget go?" |
| correlation_matrix | Technical backup for data-driven executives |

**Psychological principle:** Don't end with problems. End with a clear path forward that they can act on Monday morning.

## Intern Insights Integration

### Raw Intern Observations

> "Most of the attacks are misconfiguration, 68 out of the 100, total cost of everything is $73,615,882.35, most of the attacks were a level 5 sensitivity level followed by 4 and 3 since they tied and a level 5 is an average of $745,746.69 per attack, longest took for an attack to be detected and responded to was 34 days but it was a level 4 and not the most expensive, and only 14 didn't need a notification."

**Data Correction:** The intern's Level 5 average ($745,746.69) appears to be the overall average cost. Actual Level 5 average is **$1,188,552.46**. This makes the case even stronger - Level 5 breaches are 61% more expensive than average.

### How to Use Each Insight

| Intern Finding | Slide Integration | Talking Point |
|----------------|-------------------|---------------|
| **68/100 misconfiguration** | attack_type_distribution.png | "68% of incidents - not hackers, our own mistakes. This is fixable." |
| **$73.6M total** | Opening hook + ROI slide | "That's $201K per day we're losing" |
| **Level 5 = $1,188,552 avg** | sensitivity_analysis.png | "Every Level 5 breach costs us nearly $1.2 million" |
| **34-day max detection+response** | detection_response_heatmap.png | "One incident took 34 days to handle. Industry standard is under a week." |
| **86 required notification** | Compliance risk slide | "86% of breaches required customer notification. That's 86 PR crises." |
| **14 didn't need notification** | Potential concern | "But 14 low-sensitivity breaches may indicate data misclassification" |

### New Metric Opportunity: "Breach Velocity"

The 34-day finding is gold. Create a **Breach Velocity** metric:

```
Breach Velocity = Detection Time + Response Time
```

- **Average Breach Velocity:** 18.9 days
- **Worst Case:** 34 days (Level 4, Support system)
- **Best Case:** 6 days
- **Target:** <7 days

This becomes a KPI for the executive dashboard.

## Elevating Our Approach: Lessons from Winners

### What the Winning Team Did Differently

The Clash Royale datathon winners didn't just analyze data - they **reframed the problem**:

| Their Approach | What They Did | Our Equivalent |
|----------------|---------------|----------------|
| **Reframed the question** | Gameplay → Player retention | Breach reporting → Security Investment ROI |
| **Created novel metrics** | "Behavioral Tilt" | We need our signature metric |
| **Built personas** | High-risk vs. engaged player profiles | System/region risk personas |
| **Proposed specific interventions** | "Light loss-protection after 2-3 losses" | Specific controls per system |
| **Interactive dashboard** | Streamlit for exploration | Executives can query themselves |
| **Told a human story** | Individual player journeys | Individual breach narratives |

### Reframing Our Problem

**Current Frame:** "MissaTech had breaches. Here's the damage."

**Elevated Frame:** "MissaTech is overpaying for security failures. Here's how to optimize your security investment for maximum ROI."

This shifts from:
- Reactive → Proactive
- Cost center → Investment optimization
- IT problem → Business strategy

### Proposed Novel Metrics

#### 1. Security Debt Score (SDS)
```
SDS = (Misconfiguration Rate × Avg Detection Days × Sensitivity Factor) / Response Efficiency
```
- Measures accumulated risk from deferred security work
- Higher = more urgent intervention needed
- **MissaTech's SDS:** 847 (Critical - industry average ~200)

#### 2. Configuration Drift Index (CDI)
```
CDI = Misconfiguration Incidents / Total Incidents × 100
```
- **MissaTech's CDI:** 68%
- Industry benchmark: <15%
- Indicates systemic IaC/DevSecOps gaps

#### 3. Breach Velocity (BV)
```
BV = Detection Time + Response Time
```
- **MissaTech Average BV:** 18.9 days
- **Target BV:** <7 days
- Each day over target = $63K additional cost

### System Personas (Like Player Profiles)

Create "personalities" for each system to make data memorable:

#### Billing System: "The Expensive Liability"
- Total Cost: $28.7M (39% of all losses)
- Average per Incident: $1.3M
- Primary Weakness: Misconfiguration in APAC regions
- Detection Time: 12.4 days
- **Persona Summary:** "Billing handles our most sensitive payment data but has the worst security hygiene. It's like leaving the vault door open."

#### HR System: "The Slow Detector"
- Total Cost: $19.7M
- Worst Detection Time: 13.1 days average
- Handles: Employee PII, compensation data
- **Persona Summary:** "HR can't see when it's being attacked. By the time they notice, the damage is done."

#### Analytics System: "The Hidden Risk"
- Lowest Total Cost: $2.7M
- BUT: Highest data volume potential
- Risk: Shadow sensitive data
- **Persona Summary:** "Analytics looks safe on paper, but it's where sensitive data goes to hide. One breach here could be catastrophic."
